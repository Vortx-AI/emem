emem

Earth memory, signed at the edge.

When an LLM is asked what is at a place, it usually guesses. It has no stable handle for the patch of ground at 19.07° N, 72.87° E, and no audit trail for the number it returns. emem is the missing handle. Every cell on Earth gets a 64-bit identifier (about 9.55 m on a side at the equator), every measurement at that cell is recorded as a fact keyed by cell, band, and time, and every fact is signed with ed25519 over the blake3 hash of its canonical CBOR. Paste the 26-character content id into a chat and your colleague can pull the same bytes from any responder and verify the signature in their browser at /verify.

The hosted responder is https://emem.dev. There are no keys, no accounts, no SaaS quotas. The corpus today covers thirty-five bands across optical, radar, terrain, weather, land cover, human geography, and three foundation embeddings (Clay v1.5, Prithvi-EO-2.0, Tessera). Forty-three upstream sources back the materialiser; the catalogue at /v1/materializers lists which ones are wired on this node. One hundred and fifty-five composition recipes turn raw bands into named scores (flood risk, walkability, biodiversity proxy, carbon-MRV index, EUDR compliance) and live at /v1/algorithms. The MCP surface speaks Streamable HTTP and exposes forty-nine tools at /mcp; the same handlers answer plain REST at /openapi.json.

A first call

$ curl -sX POST https://emem.dev/v1/locate \
    -H 'content-type: application/json' \
    -d '{"q":"South Mumbai"}' | jq .cell64
"defi.zb4d9.pefa.zf619"

$ curl -sX POST https://emem.dev/v1/recall \
    -H 'content-type: application/json' \
    -d '{"cell":"defi.zb4d9.pefa.zf619","bands":["copdem30m.elevation_mean"]}' \
  | jq '.facts[0]'
{ "value": 14.0, "unit": "m_msl",
  "fact_cid": "bafy2bzaceaqxfn4obg…",
  "receipt": { "signature": "ed25519:…64-bytes…",
               "responder": "777er3yihg…" } }

The recall was answered out of the hot cache. If the cell had been cold, the responder would have fetched Copernicus DEM through its STAC connector, signed the value as its own primary attestation, persisted it, and returned in the same response. Warm reads take milliseconds. The first request to a fresh cell pays the upstream cost once, and every subsequent caller anywhere on the network sees the cached fact and the same signature.

What three encoders see

For questions of the form "is this place changing?", emem runs three independent foundation encoders over the same scene and votes. Clay v1.5 reads Sentinel-2 at a 2.6 km receptive field, Prithvi-EO-2.0 reads HLS at 6.7 km, and Tessera reads Sentinel-1 plus Sentinel-2 at the per-pixel scale. Each one carries its own aliasing pattern, so the disagreements are informative. Where all three agree, the change is real; where one says yes and two say no, the algorithm flags receptive-field aliasing rather than a land-surface event. The recipe is clay_prithvi_tessera_triple_consensus@1, with six domain variants for deforestation, wetland change, urban expansion, disaster anomaly, climate archetype, and coastal erosion. The whitepaper at /whitepaper.md walks the math.

Verifying without trusting

The responder's public key is published at /.well-known/emem.json. Every receipt names that key, the served timestamp, the primitive, the cells, and the fact ids that contributed to the answer. The browser-side verifier at /verify recomputes the canonical preimage from a receipt and checks the signature using @noble/curves. No callback to emem.dev, nothing to trust about the issuer. A receipt issued today verifies in any year against the same pubkey, regardless of whether this node is still online. Self-host a responder yourself, and the receipts you minted there stay verifiable when we are not.

Real workloads

The current calling pattern is roughly half real-estate and insurance underwriting (flood risk, walkability, elevation, surface-water recurrence) and half climate journalism and corporate disclosure (carbon-MRV, NDVI trajectories, mangrove change, supply-chain forest adjacency). Smaller share: agriculture (crop stress, soil moisture proxy, growing-season anomaly), urban planning (heat island, green-space share), disaster response (Sentinel-1 flood extent under cloud, MODIS burned area), energy siting, conservation, fisheries, tourism. The list is not aspirational. It is what calls actually look like in the last fourteen days of /v1/agent_stats.

Honest limits

We serve only open data. Sub-metre commercial imagery (Planet Pelican, Maxar) is not here; bring your own connector. The JEPA v2 dynamics head is structurally wired but the trained head is upstream-bottlenecked on multi-vintage Tessera data, so /v1/jepa_predict_v2 short-circuits to the last attested vintage with an honest untrained_baseline flag on the receipt, not a fake prediction. Tessera's own historical backfill is partial; 2024 is reliable, earlier vintages are sparse. The deployment is single-host. There is no federation, no global routing layer, no SOC 2. Bands not wired at this responder return a signed absence with a typed reason, not silence. Check /v1/materializers first.
