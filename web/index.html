emem

Earth memory for AI agents.

Every place on Earth gets a stable address called a cell64, about 10 m on a side. Facts about that place live at the address: elevation, weather, land cover, foundation embeddings, whatever a materializer has signed and put there. The algebra is Cell × Band × Tslot → Fact. A cell is the where, a band is the what (one of 34 named bands at /v1/bands), a tslot is the when. Every read comes back with an ed25519 signature and a blake3 CID over the bytes you got. Paste a CID into Slack and someone else can pull the same fact and verify it themselves. No API keys. No accounts.

Point an MCP client (Claude, Cursor, Cline, Gemini, ChatGPT) at https://emem.dev/mcp and the tools show up. Or hit plain HTTP. GET /v1/locate?place=Mount+Fuji returns the cell. GET /v1/elevation?lat=35.36&lng=138.73 returns 3709 m with its signature attached. For agents bootstrapping the surface, /v1/discover fits in 1 KB, /llms.txt and /agents.md describe the protocol in prose, /openapi.json has the full schema, the spec is at /spec.md. Run a node yourself; the receipts are still verifiable when we're not.

Three lines, end to end

curl -s https://emem.dev/v1/locate?place=Mount+Fuji
curl -s https://emem.dev/v1/elevation?lat=35.36&lng=138.73
curl -s https://emem.dev/v1/agent_stats     # operator-side aggregates, no cookies, no PII

Worked example: did this place flood?

Locate Sindh, Pakistan with POST /v1/locate, then POST /v1/recall with bands surface_water.recurrence and sentinel1_raw. The response carries two signed Facts and a Receipt. Quote the first receipt.fact_cids[0] in your answer; any agent can replay GET /v1/facts/<cid> and recompute the blake3 to verify the same number you saw. POST /v1/diff with two tslots returns a signed change between dates.

Use cases

Consumer health (CAMS air quality, urban heat, wildfire smoke), real estate and insurance (flood risk, walkability, elevation), climate finance and carbon MRV (deforestation, NDVI trajectories with signed receipts), ESG and SDG reporting (biodiversity proxies, supply-chain forest adjacency), agriculture (crop stress, evapotranspiration, growing-season anomaly), urban planning (heat island, building density, green-space share), disaster response (S1 flood extent under cloud, MODIS burned area), conservation (Hansen forest loss, mangrove change), energy siting (solar / wind / slope), mining impact, tourism (marine waves, SST, snow cover, nowcast weather), fisheries (SST, chlorophyll, bathymetry).

What emem will not answer

Real-time human movement, names of individuals at a cell, indoor environment, copyrighted satellite imagery (we serve the open-data subset), or any band a materializer is not wired for at this responder (check /v1/materializers first; an empty facts[] with materialize_notes[] is the honest "no" answer, not silence).
